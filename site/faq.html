Frequently Asked Questions

Practical answers about fees, telehealth, scheduling, and what to expect.

Fees and insurance

  • How much do sessions cost?

    Siochain Hope Counseling is private pay. Session fees are confirmed during the free consultation call. Please reach out directly for current rates.

  • Do you accept insurance?

    I'm not in-network with any insurance plans. However, I can provide a superbill — an itemized receipt with the diagnostic and billing codes your insurance needs — which you can submit for potential out-of-network reimbursement.

    Many plans cover a portion of out-of-network mental health services. It's worth calling your insurance to ask about your out-of-network benefits before we start.

  • What is a superbill and how does it work?

    A superbill is a detailed receipt I provide after each session. It includes the date of service, CPT procedure code, diagnosis code, my NPI number, and the amount you paid. You submit it to your insurance company directly, and they reimburse you according to your out-of-network benefits.

    I don't handle the insurance claim — I give you the documentation, and you submit it. Most insurance companies have an online portal or a fax number for this.

Getting started

  • Do you offer a free consultation?

    Yes. I offer a free 15-minute phone consultation before we start. It's a chance for you to ask questions, tell me a little about what's going on, and get a feel for whether we'd be a good fit. No pressure, no obligation.

  • How do I get started?

    The easiest way is to reach out through the contact form or call (501) 943-5808. We'll schedule a free 15-minute consultation, figure out whether we're a good fit, and if so, set up your first full session.

    Scheduling and intake paperwork happen through a secure client portal. I'll send you the link once we've confirmed we're moving forward.

  • How long are sessions?

    Standard sessions are 50 minutes. EMDR processing sessions are also 50 minutes, though we may occasionally schedule extended time for intensive processing work.

    SAP evaluations are structured differently — they typically take 1 to 2 hours depending on the required components.

Telehealth

  • Do you offer telehealth?

    Yes. I offer telehealth to anyone in Pennsylvania. Sessions happen through a HIPAA-compliant video platform — all you need is a private space and a reliable internet connection.

    Telehealth is available for individual therapy, EMDR, and play therapy (with some adjustments for younger children).

  • Can EMDR be done over telehealth?

    Yes. Telehealth EMDR has solid research support. Instead of following a light bar, we use bilateral stimulation adapted for video — tapping or auditory cues. Research shows outcomes are comparable to in-person sessions.

    You'll need a private space, a reliable internet connection, and ideally a device large enough to see each other clearly.

About specific services

  • What is a SAP evaluation and do I need one?

    A SAP (Substance Abuse Professional) evaluation is a federally mandated assessment for DOT-regulated employees who have violated drug or alcohol testing requirements. If you're a commercial driver, pilot, railroad employee, or work in another safety-sensitive DOT-regulated role and you've tested positive or refused a test, you're required to complete a SAP evaluation before returning to that work.

    I'm DATIA-certified and serve Pennsylvania and Delaware. See the SAP Evaluations page for more detail.

  • Do you work with children?

    Yes. I offer play therapy for children ages 3 to 12. Play therapy uses play — the child's natural language — to help kids work through anxiety, trauma, grief, family transitions, and behavioral changes.

    Parents are kept informed throughout the process. See the Play Therapy page for more information, including what parent involvement looks like.

Crisis and safety

  • What should I do if I'm in crisis?

    Siochain Hope Counseling is not a crisis service. If you are in crisis or danger, please contact one of the following immediately:

    • 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline — call or text 988
    • Crisis Text Line — text HOME to 741741
    • 911 — if you are in immediate danger

    These services are available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
