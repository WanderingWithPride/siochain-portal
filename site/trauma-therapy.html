Trauma Therapy

Healing isn't linear. Neither is good therapy. West Chester, PA — in-person and telehealth across Pennsylvania.

What is trauma therapy?

Trauma therapy is specifically designed to help people process and heal from traumatic experiences — not just talk about them. It's different from general therapy in that it's structured around how trauma actually lives in the body and brain: as stored activation, not just memories.

A lot of people come in thinking they need to retell everything from the beginning before anything can get better. You don't. Good trauma therapy starts with safety and stabilization — learning to feel grounded before going anywhere near the hard stuff. The pace is yours.

Trauma-informed therapy recognizes that your responses make sense. The numbness, the hypervigilance, the difficulty trusting — those are adaptations, not defects. We work with that, not against it.

Maura's approach

I work from a trauma-informed foundation — which means I'm always thinking about safety, choice, and pace. I don't push. I don't have a timeline in my head for when you "should" be better. I follow your lead.

Depending on what you're working through, I may use elements of cognitive behavioral therapy, somatic awareness, and EMDR. We'll talk about what feels right for you before we use any approach. Nothing happens by surprise.

LGBTQIA+ and polyamorous clients are welcome. I understand that trauma can be compounded by systems and environments that weren't built with you in mind.

Who trauma therapy can help

Trauma looks different for everyone. You don't need a diagnosis or a "big enough" event to deserve support.

Types of trauma

  • PTSD and complex PTSD (C-PTSD)
  • Childhood trauma and adverse experiences
  • Relationship trauma and emotional abuse
  • Medical trauma and traumatic diagnoses
  • Grief and loss
  • Community and collective trauma

Common reasons people reach out

  • Nightmares or intrusive memories
  • Difficulty feeling safe, even when you are
  • Emotional numbness or disconnection
  • Avoidance of people, places, or situations
  • "I know I'm fine but I don't feel fine"
  • Physical symptoms with no clear medical cause

What to expect

The first session is mostly conversation. I want to understand what's bringing you in and what you're hoping for. There's no intake questionnaire on the first call — just two people figuring out if this feels like a good fit.

From there, we build at your pace. Early sessions often focus on stabilization: grounding techniques, understanding your nervous system, and making sure you have tools to use between sessions. We don't dive into the deep end before you're ready.

Sessions are 50 minutes. Available in-person in West Chester, PA, or via secure telehealth anywhere in Pennsylvania.

Questions about trauma therapy

  • What is trauma therapy?

    Trauma therapy is a type of therapy that focuses specifically on helping people process and heal from traumatic experiences. It uses evidence-based approaches — like trauma-informed cognitive behavioral therapy and EMDR — to help your nervous system process what happened, rather than just talking about it abstractly.

  • How long does trauma therapy take?

    There's no universal answer, because trauma isn't universal. Some people work through a specific event in a few months. Others are processing complex, layered experiences that take longer. We'll check in regularly about where you are and what's working. You're not locked into anything.

  • Is telehealth okay for trauma work?

    Yes — telehealth trauma therapy is effective and, for many people, actually preferable. Being in your own space can feel safer. We use a secure HIPAA-compliant platform, and we'll make sure you're in a private, comfortable environment before each session.

  • How is trauma therapy different from regular therapy?

    Trauma therapy is specifically structured around what happens in your nervous system when you've experienced trauma — not just how you think about it. It's slower and more intentional about pacing. We build safety and stabilization first, before any deeper processing work begins.
