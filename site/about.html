About Maura

Licensed Clinical Social Worker  ·  West Chester, PA

Meet Maura

I started this practice because I believe everyone deserves care that actually fits them.

I'm Maura Rupp, a Licensed Clinical Social Worker based in West Chester, Pennsylvania. I've spent nearly a decade doing clinical work in outpatient mental health and behavioral health settings in southeastern PA — working with people navigating anxiety, trauma, addiction, chronic illness, and the complicated intersection of physical and mental health.

I started Siochain Hope Counseling because I wanted to create a practice that felt different. Somewhere that isn't transactional, isn't rushed, and isn't trying to fit you into a template. Somewhere that takes its time.

"Siochain" is an Irish word that means peace. That's what we're working toward.

My approach

I work from a trauma-informed foundation — which means I'm thinking about safety, pacing, and your right to make choices about your own care in everything we do. I'm collaborative, not directive. You're the expert on your own life. I bring clinical tools and a clear head. Together, we figure out what's actually useful.

I don't believe in a one-size-fits-all approach to therapy. I might draw on cognitive behavioral techniques with one person, EMDR with another, and a more relational, exploratory style with a third. I follow your lead and tell you what I'm thinking. Nothing gets decided without you.

I'm direct without being blunt. I'll gently push back when I think it will help, and I'll tell you what I notice — not just reflect your words back at you. I find that most people appreciate that.

LGBTQIA+ affirming. Polyamorous and non-traditional relationship structures welcome. I'm not going to be surprised by your life.

Who I work with

I work with a few distinct populations:

  • Adults navigating anxiety, trauma, depression, grief, life transitions, chronic illness, and the aftermath of difficult experiences
  • Children (play therapy) — ages 3 to 12 — working through anxiety, behavioral changes, trauma, grief, and family transitions
  • DOT-regulated employees who need a SAP evaluation to return to safety-sensitive work, including commercial drivers throughout Pennsylvania and Delaware
  • People living with chronic illness — the grief, identity disruption, medical trauma, and exhaustion that come with it
  • Caregivers who are supporting a family member with illness or disability and need their own space

Location and telehealth

Siochain Hope Counseling is based in West Chester, Pennsylvania (Chester County). I offer telehealth sessions to anyone in Pennsylvania — on your schedule, from wherever you are.

For SAP evaluations, I serve Pennsylvania and Delaware.

I am licensed to practice in Pennsylvania only. If you're located outside of PA and interested in telehealth, feel free to reach out — but I'll need to confirm whether I can work with you legally based on your location.
