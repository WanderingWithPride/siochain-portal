Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) Evaluations

DOT-required evaluations for commercial drivers and DOT-regulated employees. Serving Chester County, PA and Delaware. Fast scheduling available.

What is a SAP evaluation, and who needs one?

If you work in a DOT-regulated industry and you've had a drug or alcohol policy violation — a positive test, a refusal to test, or a documented violation — federal regulations require you to be evaluated by a qualified Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) before you can return to safety-sensitive duties.

This isn't optional, and it's not something your employer or an HR department can skip. It's a federal requirement under 49 CFR Part 40.

A SAP is a licensed professional — in this case, a Licensed Clinical Social Worker — who is specifically trained and listed in the DOT/DATIA SAP registry. The SAP evaluates your situation, determines what treatment or education you need, and writes a formal recommendation that you and your employer receive.

Who this applies to

DOT safety-sensitive positions include:

  • Commercial motor vehicle drivers (CDL holders, truckers)
  • Transit workers (bus drivers, rail operators)
  • Aviation workers (pilots, air traffic controllers, crew)
  • Maritime workers (merchant mariners)
  • Pipeline workers
  • Federal railroad employees

If you're not sure whether your position qualifies, call us. We'll give you a straight answer.

How the SAP process works

1

Initial evaluation

A face-to-face clinical assessment (in-person or telehealth) to review the circumstances of the violation and your history. After this, I provide a written SAP report with my recommendation for treatment or education.

2

Complete the recommendation

You complete whatever treatment, counseling, or education the SAP recommends. This might be a few sessions of outpatient counseling, a structured education program, or more intensive care depending on your situation.

3

Follow-up evaluation

After completing the recommended program, you return for a follow-up evaluation. If you've met the requirements, I provide clearance documentation to your employer's Medical Review Officer (MRO) so you can return to safety-sensitive work.

Why a qualified SAP matters

Not every counselor or therapist can perform a SAP evaluation. To conduct DOT SAP evaluations, a provider must have specific training and certification recognized by the DOT and DATIA (Drug and Alcohol Testing Industry Association).

Maura Rupp is SAP certified and listed to conduct evaluations for Pennsylvania and Delaware residents. She understands what the federal process requires and can move efficiently so you're not waiting longer than necessary to get back to work.

The SAP evaluation is not designed to get you in trouble. It's a federally mandated pathway back to work. The SAP's job is to evaluate honestly and recommend what you actually need — not to punish you.

Serving Pennsylvania and Delaware

SAP evaluations are available to drivers and employees in Chester County, PA and throughout Pennsylvania and Delaware. Telehealth evaluations are available where permitted under DOT guidelines.

West Chester is conveniently located near major transportation corridors in the region, including Route 30, Route 202, and I-95 — making in-person appointments accessible for drivers throughout the area.

How to schedule a SAP evaluation

Call or email directly. SAP evaluations are time-sensitive — we prioritize prompt scheduling because we understand you have a job to get back to.

When you reach out, have the following ready if possible:

  • Your name, employer, and type of DOT-regulated position
  • The nature of the violation (positive test, refusal, etc.)
  • Any paperwork from your employer or MRO

Common questions

  • What is a SAP evaluation?

    A Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) evaluation is a federally required assessment for DOT-regulated employees who have violated a drug or alcohol policy. A qualified SAP evaluates the employee, determines what treatment or education is needed, and provides a formal recommendation. The employee must complete these requirements before returning to safety-sensitive duties.

  • How much does a SAP evaluation cost?

    SAP evaluation fees vary. Contact us directly for current pricing. The evaluation, follow-up assessment, and any required treatment are typically the employee's responsibility, though some employers cover the cost. Contact us for current rates.

  • How soon can I get scheduled?

    We make every effort to schedule SAP evaluations quickly, as we understand drivers and employees are often waiting to return to work. Call or email directly for availability — we prioritize prompt scheduling for SAP cases.

  • What happens after the SAP evaluation?

    After the initial evaluation, the SAP provides a written recommendation for treatment or education. Once you complete those requirements, you return to the SAP for a follow-up evaluation. The SAP then determines whether you are ready to return to safety-sensitive duties and communicates this to your employer's Medical Review Officer (MRO).
