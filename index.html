Privacy Policy — Siochain Hope Counseling, LLC

Siochain Hope Counseling, LLC (“we,” “us,” or “our”) is committed to protecting the privacy of all visitors to this website and all clients of our practice. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and the choices you have.

This website does not use advertising trackers, retargeting pixels, or third-party analytics services that build profiles about you. We may use a single first-party cookie to remember your preference for dismissing the cookie notice. No personally identifiable information is collected through that cookie.

If you complete a contact form on this website, we collect the information you provide (such as your name and email address) solely for the purpose of responding to your inquiry. We do not sell, trade, or share your contact information with any third party.

All clinical records and protected health information are handled in accordance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA). Our Notice of Privacy Practices, which describes how we use and protect your health information as a client, is available separately on this website.

This website is hosted on Squarespace. Squarespace may collect standard server logs (such as IP addresses and page views) as part of its hosting infrastructure. These logs are not shared with Siochain Hope Counseling and are governed by Squarespace’s own privacy policy.

If you communicate with us by email, please be aware that standard email is not a fully secure channel. Do not send sensitive health information via email. For secure communication as an existing client, please use the messaging feature within our practice management platform (SimplePractice).

You have the right to request access to any personal information we hold about you, to request correction of inaccurate information, and to request deletion of your information where no legal obligation requires us to retain it. To make any of these requests, contact us at mrupp@siochainhopecounseling.com.

This policy may be updated periodically. The date of the most recent update appears at the bottom of this page. Continued use of this website following any update constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy.

THIS NOTICE DESCRIBES HOW MEDICAL INFORMATION ABOUT YOU MAY BE USED AND DISCLOSED AND HOW YOU CAN GET ACCESS TO THIS INFORMATION. PLEASE REVIEW IT CAREFULLY.

Our Duty to Safeguard Your Protected Health Information. We are required by law to maintain the privacy of your protected health information (PHI) and to provide you with this notice of our legal duties and privacy practices. We are required to abide by the terms of this notice while it is in effect.

How We May Use and Disclose Your Health Information. We use and disclose your health information for treatment, payment, and health care operations. For treatment, we may share your information with other health care providers involved in your care. For payment, we may share your information as needed to process a claim or obtain reimbursement. For health care operations, we may use your information to evaluate the quality of care we provide.

Uses and Disclosures Requiring Your Authorization. We will obtain your written authorization before using or disclosing your health information for any purpose not described in this notice, including most uses of psychotherapy notes, marketing, and sale of your information.

Special Protections for Psychotherapy Notes. Your psychotherapy notes (private notes kept by your therapist separate from your general medical record) receive extra protections under HIPAA. We will not disclose psychotherapy notes without your written authorization, except as required by law.

Your Rights Regarding Your Health Information. You have the right to inspect and copy your health information; to request a restriction on certain uses or disclosures; to request that we communicate with you in a particular way or at a particular location; to request an amendment to your record; to receive an accounting of disclosures; and to receive a paper copy of this notice.

Required Disclosures. We are required to disclose your health information when required by law, including to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to your health or safety or the health or safety of another person, or when required by a court order.

Complaints. If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with us or with the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. I'm Maura Rupp, a Licensed Clinical Social Worker (LCSW) in Pennsylvania. I work with adults and children navigating trauma, anxiety, depression, grief, chronic illness, and life transitions. My approach is trauma-informed at its core, which means I understand that what happened to you matters — and that healing isn't about willpower or "getting over it." It's about understanding your nervous system, rebuilding your sense of safety, and moving forward at a pace that actually works for your life.

I don't work from a script. Therapy isn't one-size-fits-all, and the most effective work happens when we build a relationship where you feel genuinely heard rather than processed. I draw from multiple evidence-based approaches — EMDR, cognitive-behavioral therapy, mindfulness, attachment-based work, and play therapy — because different people need different tools. My job is to figure out what fits you, not to squeeze you into a method.

One of the most powerful tools I use is EMDR — Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing. EMDR was developed specifically to treat trauma and PTSD, and it has one of the strongest evidence bases in mental health treatment. It works by helping the brain fully process distressing memories that have become "stuck," reducing their emotional intensity without requiring you to talk through every detail in depth. EMDR can help with trauma from childhood, accidents, medical experiences, sexual abuse, grief, and anxiety — and it often produces results faster than traditional talk therapy alone. If you've tried therapy before and felt like you were circling the same material, EMDR may offer a different path through.

I also specialize in chronic illness and the emotional toll that living in an unpredictable body takes on a person. When you're managing a long-term medical condition, the mental health piece is rarely addressed with the same seriousness as the physical one — but it should be. Chronic illness brings grief, identity disruption, medical anxiety, relationship strain, and sometimes real trauma from difficult diagnoses and procedures. I understand this intersection not only clinically, but personally. If you've ever been told "it's just stress" by a doctor who didn't listen, or felt like no one in your life quite understands what you're carrying — that's exactly the kind of space I try to create here.

For clients in safety-sensitive positions who need a Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) evaluation, I provide DOT-compliant SAP evaluations for Pennsylvania and Delaware residents. These evaluations are required for return-to-duty determinations following a violation of federal DOT drug and alcohol regulations.

I work with children using play therapy — a developmentally appropriate approach that allows children to communicate and process their experiences through play rather than words. Through play therapy, children can work through anxiety, behavioral challenges, family transitions, trauma, and grief in a way that feels safe and natural to them. Parents are partners in this process.

You don't have to have it figured out to reach out. I offer a brief phone consultation so we can talk before you commit to anything. I'd love to hear what's bringing you here.

DOT Substance Abuse Professional (SAP) Evaluations

If you or an employee has received a federal DOT drug or alcohol violation, a SAP evaluation is the required first step in the return-to-duty process. Federal law (49 CFR Part 40) mandates that any employee in a safety-sensitive DOT-regulated position — including trucking, aviation, transit, railroad, and pipeline — must complete a SAP evaluation before returning to duty. This is not optional.
